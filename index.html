Bulgaria

By George Pernov

The Capital: Sofia

With a population of about 1.5 million people, Bulgaria's capital, Sofia, is home to about 20% of the country's total population. Sofia has been continuously inhabited since at least 7000 BC, making it one of the oldest cities in the world.

Eastern Orthodox Monasteries

One of the things Bulgaria is known for is its many Eastern Orthodox monasteries. The largest, pictured here is the Rila Monastery. Founded in 927 AD, the Rila Monastery is one of Bulgaria's most important cultural and historical monuments.

Amazing Scenery

Bulgaria is also known for its stunning scenery. Its green mountains and hills and clear bodies of water have been relatively untouched by pollution and industry. Bulgaria is a great destination for anyone who wants to vacation in an area with incredible natural scenery.

Rich History

Bulgaria has a long, rich history as a country. Its two largest cities, Sofia and Plovdiv, have been inhabited for over 8000 years. It has been a part of various empires throughout history.
